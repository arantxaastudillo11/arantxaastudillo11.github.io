Paintings and Drawings


Majestic

Arantxa Astudillo
Charcoal on Wood
36X24''
2017

I spent two weeks in Thailand in November 2016. I saw Asian elephants get beaten until they bled. I also saw them at a sanctuary, with their scars and their crooked (broken) legs. Asian elephants have been tragically hurt by people who force them to pull trees from the ground.



Self Identity

Arantxa Astudillo
Charcoal on Paper
36X24''
2017

Like faces, hands are unique to each individual. They age with us and are used to express ourselves when we speak. I drew my own hand relaxed. As I age I will know how my hand looked when I created this drawing.

Tatara Abuela

Arantxa Astudillo
Acrylic on Canvas
30X30''
2016

Tatara abuela means great grandmother in Spanish. I never knew my late great grandmother. One day, I began painting as a meditation exercise. I painted this woman who looks just like my late great grandmother, a smoker.

Zeus

Arantxa Astudillo
Acrylic on Canvas
75X30''
2015

After a trip to Greece, I was inspired by the beautiful art I saw. I went home and recreated one of my favorite statues, Zeus, afterwards, I decided to paint his lover, Hera.

Hera

Arantxa Astudillo
Acrylic on Canvas
75X30''
2015

I painted Hera so that my painting of Zeus would have his lover by his side.
